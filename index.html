Philosophy & Research

My work explores the concept of 'art as an ecosystem'—a living, breathing system that interacts with its environment and audience. I build installations that are not static but are in a constant state of flux, reacting to data, human presence, or natural phenomena. This approach challenges traditional notions of authorship, turning the artwork into a collaborative entity.

I treat data not just as a source of information but as a raw, expressive medium. From the magnetic resonance of brain scans to the flickering patterns of a flame, I seek to uncover the hidden aesthetics within complex datasets. My process involves translating this data into sensory experiences—sound, light, and form—that offer new perspectives on the world around us.

About the Artist

Haraldur Karlsson is an Icelandic interactive media artist and researcher. His work spans video installations, generative art, and data-driven sculptures. He holds an MFA in Digital Arts and has exhibited his work internationally, including at the Venice Biennale, Ars Electronica, and HEK (House of Electronic Arts Basel).

His research focuses on the intersection of art, technology, and science, with a particular interest in creating systems that reveal unseen patterns in our environment.
